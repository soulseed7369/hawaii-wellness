## PART 1 — STRATEGY

### The Core Thesis

You have 700+ listings already in the database. Many of these practitioners don't know they're listed. The soft launch is not about getting strangers to sign up for something new — it's about reaching people who *already have a presence on your site* and inviting them to take ownership of it. This is a fundamentally different pitch than "join our directory." It's: "You're already here. Come make it yours."

This reframe matters because:

- It eliminates the "why should I bother" objection — they're already visible to searchers
- It creates mild urgency — someone else could see outdated info
- It positions claiming as *protecting their reputation*, not doing you a favor
- It makes the ask tiny: "just verify it's you" rather than "build a profile from scratch"

### Who to Target First

**Tier 1 (Days 1–2): Warm network + high-profile practitioners**
Start with anyone you know personally or through one degree of separation. These are your founding champions. You need 15–20 claimed listings before you contact strangers, because social proof compounds. A practitioner checking the site who sees other real, claimed profiles will convert at 3–5x the rate of one who sees only pipeline-scraped stubs.

**Tier 2 (Days 2–4): Email-contactable Big Island listings with websites**
These are practitioners who have both an email and a website in the database. They're digitally active, understand online presence, and the claim flow works instantly for them (email OTP match → one-click claim). Your campaign_outreach table already segments these.

**Tier 3 (Days 4–6): Phone-contactable listings without email**
These require a personal text or DM. Higher effort per contact, but phone-only practitioners are often the most established local healers — lomilomi, Hawaiian healing, acupuncture elders. Landing one of these is worth five yoga instructors for credibility.

**Tier 4 (Days 6–7): Social-only contacts (Instagram DMs)**
Practitioners discoverable only through social media. Lower conversion rate but builds awareness. Good for younger practitioners (breathwork, sound healing, yoga).

### Why This Sequence Works

The sequence is designed to build **visible momentum before scaling outreach**:

1. Warm contacts claim first → you get real profiles with photos and bios
2. Those profiles make the directory look alive when cold contacts check the site
3. Each claimed profile adds a potential referral source ("know anyone else who should be on here?")
4. By day 4–5, you have enough social proof to send bolder outreach

### The Message Angle That Gets Claims

Don't lead with "we built a directory, please join." Lead with:

> "Your practice is already showing up on HawaiiWellness.net — I wanted to make sure the info is accurate. You can claim it for free in about 2 minutes."

This works because:

- It's informational, not salesy
- It implies they should care (their info is public)
- "Claim" feels like protecting something, not signing up for something
- "2 minutes" removes the effort objection
- "Free" removes the money objection

### How to Create Momentum With Early Wins

- Get 5 claims on Day 1 from warm contacts. Text them personally.
- Screenshot the first few completed profiles and share in your own networks ("look who just joined")
- Ask each claimer: "Do you know 2–3 other practitioners who should be on here?" (this is your single most important growth lever)
- By Day 3, you want 15–20 claims. This is the tipping point where the directory feels real.

### What NOT to Do in the Soft Launch

- **Don't mention paid plans in outreach.** Zero. If they ask, say "we have optional upgrades later, but the free listing is the real thing."
- **Don't blast emails.** Max 30–50 personalized emails per day. Quality over quantity.
- **Don't use marketing language.** No "unlock your potential" or "grow your practice." Keep it neighbor-to-neighbor.
- **Don't contact Maui/Oahu/Kauai yet.** Stay focused on Big Island until you have 50+ claims.
- **Don't post on social media until Day 3+.** You need real profiles on the site first.
- **Don't ask people to "build a profile."** Ask them to "claim" or "verify" their existing listing. The profile building happens naturally after claim.

## PART 2 — PRIORITIZED TARGET SEGMENTS

### Segment 1: Personal Network (Warm Contacts)

**Why first:** Trust is pre-built. These people will claim because *you* asked, not because the value prop convinced them. They become your founding cohort and referral engine.

**Likely objections:** "I'll do it later" (low urgency). Counter with: "Takes 2 minutes — I can walk you through it right now if you want."

**Best channel:** Personal text message from your phone.

**Value prop:** "I'm launching this and you'd be one of the first practitioners on it. Your profile is already there — just needs you to claim it."

**Outreach type:** Personal text, then phone call if no response in 24 hours.

### Segment 2: Established Practitioners With Websites + Email (Big Island)

**Why second:** Digitally savvy, understand SEO/visibility, have complete-ish listings from pipeline. Email-match claim flow works instantly. These are chiropractors, acupuncturists, naturopaths, therapists — people who already market online.

**Likely objections:** "What's the catch?" / "Is this another directory that charges later?" Counter with: "The free listing is permanent. We'll have optional paid upgrades later for people who want extra visibility, but the core listing stays free forever."

**Best channel:** Email first, follow-up text if no response in 48 hours.

**Value prop:** "Your practice is already showing up in our Big Island wellness directory. I wanted to reach out so you can verify the info and make it yours — takes about 2 minutes, completely free."

**Outreach type:** Email (personalizable at scale via GPT-5.4), text follow-up.

### Segment 3: Phone-Only Practitioners (No Email in DB)

**Why third:** Often the most credible, established local healers. Lomilomi practitioners, Hawaiian healing, long-time massage therapists. Having them on the platform adds enormous legitimacy. But the claim flow requires document upload (no email match), so it's higher friction.

**Likely objections:** "I don't do internet stuff" / "I get my clients through word of mouth." Counter with: "Totally understand. People are searching online for exactly what you do on the Big Island. This just makes sure they can find you. I can help you set it up."

**Best channel:** Personal text or phone call.

**Value prop:** "People are searching for [their modality] on the Big Island. I wanted to make sure they can find you. Your name is already in our directory — I just need you to confirm it's accurate."

**Outreach type:** Text first, phone call follow-up. Offer to do the profile update for them manually if they're not tech-savvy.

### Segment 4: Instagram/Social-Active Practitioners

**Why fourth:** Younger practitioners (breathwork, sound healing, yoga, meditation) who live on Instagram but may not have a website. DMs are natural for them. Lower conversion rate but builds awareness and long-term pipeline.

**Likely objections:** "I already have Instagram, why do I need this?" Counter with: "Instagram is great for your existing followers. This helps new people searching for [modality] on the Big Island find you for the first time."

**Best channel:** Instagram DM.

**Value prop:** "Hey! I'm building a wellness directory for the Big Island and your work came up. Would love to feature you — it's free and takes like 2 minutes to claim your listing."

**Outreach type:** DM, follow-up DM in 3 days if no response.

### Segment 5: Wellness Centers, Spas, and Retreat Centers

**Why fifth (not first):** Centers are higher-value listings but slower to convert — they have front desk staff, managers, decision-makers who need to approve things. Start with individual practitioners for speed, then approach centers once you have momentum.

**Likely objections:** "I need to check with my manager/owner." Counter with: "Totally, here's a link they can look at. It's free and takes about 5 minutes. Happy to jump on a quick call if that helps."

**Best channel:** Email to business email, follow-up phone call.

**Value prop:** "Your center is already listed on HawaiiWellness.net — the Big Island's wellness directory. Claiming your free listing lets you update hours, add photos, and make sure visitors can find you."

**Outreach type:** Email, phone follow-up.

## PART 4 — CLAIM-FLOW FUNNEL AUDIT

### What I Found in the Codebase

I audited the actual claim flow in your code. Here's what exists and what needs attention:

**The flow that works:**
1. Practitioner visits their listing page → sees "Claim this listing" button
2. Clicks claim → redirected to `/claim/{listing_id}`
3. If their email matches the listing email → OTP sent → 6-digit code → instant claim
4. If email doesn't match → document upload (PDF/JPG/PNG) → admin reviews in 1–2 days
5. After claim → redirected to dashboard → can edit profile immediately

**The flow for new signups (no existing listing):**
1. Visit `/list-your-practice` → choose Free plan → redirected to `/auth`
2. Sign in via magic link → redirected to `/dashboard`
3. Onboarding checklist: complete profile, verify contact, choose plan
4. Fill out profile form → published immediately

### Friction Points Identified

**Critical (fix before launch):**

1. **No discovery path from outreach to claim.** When you text someone "your listing is on HawaiiWellness.net," they'll go to the homepage, not their specific listing. They need a direct link to their listing with the claim button visible. **Fix:** Include a direct link to their listing page in every outreach message. Format: `hawaiiwellness.net/practitioner/{slug-or-id}`. Verify these URLs work.

2. **Phone-only practitioners can't claim easily.** The email OTP path requires their email to match. The phone OTP is commented out (Twilio pending). Phone-only practitioners must use document upload, which takes 1–2 days for admin review. **Workaround:** For high-priority phone-only contacts, offer to manually link their account in the admin panel after they sign up. Text them: "Just sign up at [link] and I'll connect your listing within the hour."

3. **Magic link emails may hit spam.** Supabase OTP emails from default sender can land in spam/promotions. **Fix:** Test magic link delivery to Gmail, Yahoo, iCloud, and Outlook. If delivery is poor, add a note in outreach: "Check your spam folder for the sign-in link."

**Important (fix in first 3 days):**

4. **No "you've been listed" notification.** Pipeline-ingested practitioners don't know they exist on the site. The first they'll hear is your outreach. This is fine for the soft launch (founder-led outreach IS the notification), but you'll need automated notifications for scale.

5. **Profile completion is not incentivized after claim.** The dashboard shows a completeness percentage but there's no clear payoff for completing it. "Complete profiles get 3x more views" or similar would help. Consider adding this copy to the dashboard.

6. **The ListYourPractice page leads with pricing tiers.** For the soft launch, the pricing page shouldn't be the primary landing. Outreach should link to the practitioner's specific listing page (claim path), not the pricing page (signup path).

7. **Mobile claim flow UX.** Most practitioners will open your text on their phone. The claim flow should be tested end-to-end on mobile Safari and Chrome. Verify: magic link opens correctly on mobile, OTP entry works, dashboard is usable on small screens.

**Nice-to-have (Week 2+):**

8. **No auto-linking of new signups to existing draft listings.** If a practitioner signs up fresh (instead of claiming), they create a duplicate listing. The existing pipeline listing stays as an orphan. Consider adding email-match auto-detection during signup: "We found an existing listing that may be yours — want to claim it?"

9. **Social proof on claim page.** The claim page could show "47 Big Island practitioners have already claimed their listing" once you have numbers. This dramatically improves conversion.

10. **"What happens after I claim?" preview.** Showing a before/after of a claimed profile (stub vs. complete) on the claim page would reduce uncertainty.

### Minimum Viable Fixes Before Launch

| # | Fix | Effort | Impact |
|---|-----|--------|--------|
| 1 | Test direct listing URLs work and include them in outreach | 30 min | Critical |
| 2 | Test magic link email delivery on major providers | 30 min | Critical |
| 3 | Test full claim flow on mobile (iPhone Safari + Android Chrome) | 30 min | Critical |
| 4 | Prepare manual admin-linking process for phone-only practitioners | 15 min | High |
| 5 | Add "check spam" note to auth confirmation screen copy | 15 min | Medium |

### Manual Fallback Options for Launch Week

- **If email OTP doesn't arrive:** Tell the practitioner to check spam. If still nothing, have them sign up with a password instead, then you manually link their account in admin.
- **If phone-only practitioners can't do document upload:** They sign up, you link their listing via admin panel within the hour. Text them: "All set — your profile is live!"
- **If a practitioner wants to claim but the flow is confusing:** Jump on a 5-minute phone call and walk them through it. This is high-touch but it's launch week — every claim matters.
- **If someone creates a duplicate instead of claiming:** You merge the listings manually in admin. Pipeline listing gets deleted, their new listing stays.

## PART 6 — OFFER POSITIONING

### What Exactly Are They Getting for Free?

Frame it clearly:

> **Your free listing includes:**
> - Your practice name, location, and contact info visible to anyone searching for wellness on the Big Island
> - Your modalities listed so people can find you by what you do (massage, acupuncture, yoga, etc.)
> - A profile photo
> - A bio (up to 250 characters)
> - Direct contact — no booking fees, no commissions, no middleman
> - Control to update your info anytime from your dashboard
> - Permanent — the free listing never expires

### Strongest Emotional Reason to Claim Now

"People are already searching for what you do on the Big Island. This makes sure they find *you*."

The emotional core: **visibility to the people who need you.** Most practitioners got into wellness to help people. The idea that someone is searching for their exact modality and can't find them — that's the motivator. It's not about business growth. It's about connection.

### Strongest Practical Reasons to Claim Now

1. Your info might be wrong — claim it to fix it
2. It's free and takes 2 minutes
3. You control it — update anytime
4. No commissions, no booking fees, no catch
5. Being early means your profile is more established when the directory grows

### How to Mention Premium Without Distracting

Only mention paid plans if directly asked. The script:

> "The free listing is the real product — it's permanent and covers everything most practitioners need. Down the road, we'll have optional upgrades for things like testimonials, extra photos, and homepage features. But honestly, the free listing does the job for most people."

This works because: it validates the free tier, reduces anxiety about bait-and-switch, and plants the premium seed without pressure.

### Framing: "Claim Your Listing"

Use **"claim your listing"** as the primary CTA. Here's why:

- "Claim" implies ownership and control (positive)
- "Claim" implies something already exists (reduces perceived effort)
- "Your listing" implies it belongs to them (psychological ownership)
- Alternatives are weaker: "activate your profile" sounds corporate, "get listed" implies they're not listed yet, "sign up" sounds like commitment

Secondary framing for warm contacts: "make it yours" — casual, personal.

6. **profile_complete** — Claimed + filled out profile (photo, bio, etc.)
7. **champion** — Claimed + actively referring others
8. **declined** — Said no (note the reason)
9. **unresponsive** — 2+ follow-ups, no response
10. **bounced** — Email bounced / wrong number

### Follow-Up Cadence

| Touchpoint | Timing | Channel |
|------------|--------|---------|
| First contact | Day 0 | Text, email, or DM |
| First follow-up | Day 2 | Same channel + one new channel |
| Second follow-up | Day 5 | Different angle or channel |
| Closing the loop | Day 7 | Brief, no-pressure final message |

### Priority Scoring

- **+3** Warm contact (you know them)
- **+2** Has email + website (easy claim path)
- **+2** High-credibility modality (established practice, years in business)
- **+1** Has phone number
- **+1** Has Instagram
- **+1** Referred by someone who already claimed
- **−1** No contact info except a Google Maps listing
- **−2** Off-island phone number (may have moved)

Sort your outreach list by priority score descending. Contact the highest-priority people first.

### Warm vs. Cold Separation

Create two tabs/views in the spreadsheet:

- **Tab 1: "Warm + Referral"** — Anyone you know personally, anyone referred to you by a claimer, anyone who replied positively. These get personal texts and phone calls.
- **Tab 2: "Cold Outreach"** — Pipeline contacts you've never met. These get templated (but personalized) emails. Only escalate to text/call if they're high-priority and didn't respond to email.

## PART 9 — LOCAL MOMENTUM STRATEGY

### Use Local Credibility

- **Your origin story matters.** In every outreach, you're not "a startup" — you're a person building something for the Big Island. Lead with that. "I live here, I built this because I couldn't find a good directory of wellness practitioners, and I think others have the same problem."
- **Use "we" carefully.** For outreach, "I" is better than "we" — it's more personal and honest for a solo founder. Save "we" for the website copy.
- **Reference the island, not "Hawaiʻi" generically.** "Big Island wellness directory" is more local-sounding than "Hawaiʻi wellness platform."

### Get Your First Champions

Your first 5 claimers are not just users — they're co-builders. Treat them that way:

- Text them personally after they claim: "Mahalo — you're one of the very first practitioners on the directory. Your profile looks great."
- Ask for honest feedback: "Anything confusing about the process? Anything you'd want to see improved?"
- Ask for referrals: "Who else should be on here?"
- If they share it with their network unprompted, that's your signal that the product resonates.

A practitioner who refers 3 others is worth more than 10 cold outreach claims. Nurture these people.

### Community Trust and Referrals

- **Every outreach should end with a referral ask.** Not aggressively — just: "If you know anyone else who should be listed, I'd love to hear about them."
- **Create a simple referral chain:** When someone claims via referral, text the referrer: "Hey — [Name] just claimed their listing. Thanks for the intro!"
- **The referral loop is your primary growth engine after Week 1.** Cold outreach has a ceiling; referral-sourced contacts convert 3–5x better.

### Founding Practitioner Energy

Use "founding" language sparingly but intentionally:

- In warm outreach: "You'd be one of the first practitioners on the directory."
- On the site (after 20+ claims): "Join 30+ Big Island practitioners who've already claimed their free listing."
- Do NOT use "founding member" as a formal program — it creates expectations of perks you can't deliver yet.

The energy you want: "We're building this together" — not "You're getting exclusive early access." Hawaiʻi culture values community contribution, not exclusivity.

### Charter Member Framing — Use Lightly

A light "early supporter" framing works:

> "We're starting with the Big Island and inviting the first wave of practitioners to claim their free listings. Being early means your profile is established before the directory grows."

This creates mild urgency without fake scarcity. Don't use countdown timers or "only X spots left" — that's mainland startup energy and it'll backfire in Hawaiʻi.

### Making People Feel Like They're Building Something Meaningful

The strongest emotional lever: **"People are searching for wellness care on the Big Island and can't find it."**

Position the directory as a community resource, not a business:

> "We're building something that Hawaiʻi has been missing — a real directory where people can find the care they need. Every practitioner who claims their listing makes this more useful for the whole island."

This frames claiming as a contribution, not a transaction. In Hawaiʻi, that matters enormously.

## PART 10 — RISKS AND CONTINGENCIES

### Risk 1: Low Response Rate (<10%)

**Why it might happen:** Practitioners are busy. Email goes to spam. Cold outreach from an unknown person gets ignored.

**Countermeasures:**
- Start with warm contacts (guaranteed responses).
- Use personal texts, not email, for highest-priority contacts.
- Test email deliverability on Day 0.
- If email response rate is below 5% by Day 3, shift entirely to texts and phone calls.
- Switch subject lines if opens are low.

### Risk 2: People Visit But Don't Claim

**Why it might happen:** Confusion about the process. Mobile UX issues. "I'll do it later." Fear of commitment.

**Countermeasures:**
- Make outreach messages include direct claim links, not just the homepage.
- Test the full flow on mobile before launch.
- If people say "I'll do it later," offer: "I can walk you through it in 2 minutes on the phone right now."
- Track click-to-claim conversion. If it's below 20%, there's a UX problem — investigate immediately.

### Risk 3: Too Much Founder Effort Per Claim

**Why it might happen:** Walking each person through the process manually. Phone calls take 15 minutes instead of 5.

**Countermeasures:**
- Batch your outreach (morning: send all messages; afternoon: handle replies).
- Create a 60-second Loom/video walkthrough of the claim process to share instead of doing live walkthroughs.
- Only phone-call high-priority targets. Most people should be able to claim from a text link.
- Target: under 5 minutes of founder time per claim on average.

### Risk 4: Site Has Bugs or Friction During Launch

**Why it might happen:** Edge cases in claim flow. Magic links expire. Mobile layout issues.

**Countermeasures:**
- Test everything on Day 0.
- Have your admin panel open all week so you can manually fix issues in real-time.
- Keep a "bug log" — screenshot every issue a practitioner reports.
- Manual fallback: if the flow breaks for someone, create their account yourself and text them the login.

### Risk 5: Looking Too Salesy Too Early

**Why it might happen:** Messaging accidentally emphasizes paid plans. Outreach feels like marketing.

**Countermeasures:**
- Never mention paid plans unless asked.
- Use "I" not "we" in personal outreach.
- Don't send HTML-formatted marketing emails. Plain text is more trustworthy.
- If someone asks "what's the catch," have the answer ready (see FAQ above).

### Risk 6: Contacting the Wrong People First

**Why it might happen:** Pipeline data has moved businesses, retired practitioners, or non-wellness businesses.

**Countermeasures:**
- Start with people you know (guaranteed accuracy).
- Cross-reference pipeline data with current websites before emailing.
- If a listing is clearly wrong (closed business, wrong category), skip it and mark in CRM.
- Budget for a 15–20% "dead listing" rate in cold outreach.

### Risk 7: Lack of Critical Mass

**Why it might happen:** 50 claims out of 700 listings doesn't look impressive to a site visitor browsing.

**Countermeasures:**
- The 450+ published listings are already visible to browsers. The directory looks full even without claims.
- Claimed profiles look better (photos, complete bios) — they'll naturally float to the top.
- Focus on quality over quantity: 20 fully-complete claimed profiles are more impressive than 50 stub profiles.
- Don't show a "claimed by" indicator publicly — visitors don't need to know which profiles are unclaimed.

## PART 11 — POST-LAUNCH TRANSITION

### Phase 1: Profile Completion (Week 2–3)

After the first wave of claims, shift from "get claims" to "improve profiles."

- Send personalized nudges to every claimer with incomplete profiles (missing photo, short bio, missing modalities).
- Frame it as: "Your listing is live — adding a photo helps people connect with you before they reach out."
- Offer to help: "Want me to pull a photo from your website/Instagram and add it for you?"
- Track: profile completeness % as a KPI. Target: 70%+ of claimed profiles have photo + bio by end of Week 3.

### Phase 2: Testimonials and Success Stories (Week 3–4)

Once you have 30+ complete profiles:

- Ask 3–5 of your most engaged claimers: "Has anyone reached out to you through the directory yet?" Collect any positive responses as testimonials.
- If no organic contacts yet, that's fine — the testimonial is about the claiming experience: "It was easy to set up and now I show up when people search for massage on the Big Island."
- Feature 2–3 practitioner spotlights on social media and the homepage.
- These stories become your proof when you scale to other islands.

### Phase 3: Premium Upsell (Week 4–6)

Do NOT push premium until:
- You have 50+ claimed listings
- At least 10 practitioners have complete profiles with photos
- You've gotten at least one "someone found me through your site" success story

When ready, the upsell approach:

- Soft introduction via email to all free-tier claimers: "Thanks for being part of the directory. We just launched Premium listings — here's what you'd get: [testimonials, extra photos, working hours, priority placement]. Your free listing stays exactly as it is either way."
- Do NOT frame it as "you're missing out." Frame it as "here's how to do more."
- The Kama'aina pricing (locked-in early supporter rate) is your best conversion tool. Lead with that.
- Target conversion rate: 5–10% of free claimers upgrade in the first month.

### Phase 4: Website Bundles (Month 2+)

This is the longest play. Don't rush it.

- Identify practitioners who claimed but don't have a website (you have this data).
- The pitch: "I noticed you don't have a website yet. We offer custom wellness websites designed for Big Island practitioners — $499 setup + $19/mo. Want to see some examples?"
- Only pitch to practitioners who are engaged (complete profile, responding to messages).
- This is a high-trust sale — only works after you've built the relationship.

| 3 | Quality + stories | 3 testimonials collected |
| 4 | Premium soft intro | 5% conversion to premium |
